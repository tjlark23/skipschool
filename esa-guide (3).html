What is an Education Savings Account?

An ESA is a government-funded account that puts education dollars directly in your hands instead of a school district. Instead of your tax dollars automatically going to a public school your child does not attend, a portion goes into an account you control.

You can spend that money on curriculum, tutoring, online courses, AI learning tools, enrichment programs, testing services, and more. The rules vary by state, but the core idea is the same: your family gets real money to spend on your child's education your way.

Many homeschool families have no idea this money exists, or assume it only applies to private school students. In many states, it fully covers homeschool expenses. This guide tells you exactly what your state offers and how to get it.
