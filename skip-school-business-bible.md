## 1. VISION & MISSION

### Vision

Become the go-to media brand for homeschool families who want to use AI and modern technology to give their kids a better education than traditional school ever could.

### Mission

Help homeschool parents navigate the intersection of AI and education by delivering practical tools, curated local resources, and a community that makes homeschooling easier and more effective.

### Core Thesis

Three massive forces are converging right now:

1. **Homeschooling is exploding.** Post-COVID homeschool rates jumped from ~3% to ~9% of school-aged children. Many never went back. The number continues to grow as parents lose trust in traditional education systems.

2. **AI is transforming education.** Tools like Claude, ChatGPT, Khan Academy AI, and Alpha School's AI tutoring are making it possible for any parent to deliver a world-class education at home. 88% of parents believe AI will be critical to their child's future success.

3. **Money is flowing to homeschool families.** Texas ESA (Education Savings Account) gives homeschool families $10,000 per child per year to spend on education. Multiple states are passing similar programs. This creates a massive advertising market of businesses chasing that money.

**Skip School exists at the intersection of all three.** No one is building a media brand that connects homeschool parents to AI tools, local resources, and each other. We are.

### Why "Skip School"

The name is intentionally provocative and memorable. Homeschool parents literally chose to skip the traditional school system. The .ai domain reinforces the AI-first positioning. "SkipSchool.ai" tells the entire brand story in two words and a domain extension.

## 3. TARGET AUDIENCE

### Primary Audience: Homeschool Parents

**Demographics:**
- Age: 28-50 (skews millennial and older Gen Z)
- Gender: 70%+ women (moms drive homeschool decisions)
- Education: College-educated or self-educated (value education, chose to DIY it)
- Income: Middle to upper-middle class ($60K-$200K household income)
- Location: Suburban and exurban (exactly the communities Skip School local editions will serve)
- Tech comfort: Moderate to high (they're already researching online, many use educational apps)

**Psychographics:**
- Chose homeschooling intentionally (not just a COVID reaction)
- Value independence, customization, and control over their kids' education
- Curious about AI but uncertain how to apply it practically
- Active in homeschool communities (co-ops, Facebook groups, local meetups)
- Willing to spend money on education (especially with ESA funds available)
- Hungry for practical, actionable content (not theory or philosophy)

### Secondary Audience: Homeschool-Curious Parents

Parents considering homeschooling but haven't pulled the trigger yet. They're researching, comparing options, worried about socialization and curriculum. Skip School gives them confidence that homeschooling with AI is not only viable but superior. These readers convert to homeschoolers and become core audience members.

### Who We Are NOT Targeting

- Traditional school parents who want AI tutoring supplements (different product, different brand)
- Education policy advocates or activists (we're practical, not political)
- Unschooling purists who reject all structure (our content assumes some curriculum structure)
- Adult children caring for elderly parents (that's WilCo Seniors territory)

### Advertiser Economics (Why Businesses Will Pay)

With ESA funding, homeschool families in Texas have **$10,000 per kid per year** to spend on education. Businesses chasing that money include:

- **Curriculum companies:** $500-$2,000 per family per year in spending
- **Tutoring and enrichment programs:** $100-$300/month per student (Kumon, Code Ninjas, music academies, art studios, sports academies)
- **Alpha School and AI tutoring platforms:** Priced at exactly $10,000/year to match ESA funding
- **Co-ops with tuition:** $2,000-$5,000/year per family
- **Testing centers:** Homeschool families need standardized testing for college admission
- **College prep services:** Homeschool families think about college admissions earlier and spend more
- **Educational travel companies:** Field trips, educational tours, study abroad

These businesses have high customer LTV and limited channels to reach homeschool families. Skip School becomes their primary acquisition channel.

### Voice Guidelines (Ashley's Voice)

**Tone:** Warm, practical, encouraging but not preachy. Like a smart friend who's two steps ahead of you on the homeschool journey and happy to share what she's learned.

**What it sounds like:**
- "This week I let Claude write my 7-year-old's spelling list. Here's what happened."
- "Three apps we're actually using right now (and two I deleted after a week)."
- "I spent $0 on curriculum this month. Here's exactly how."

**What it does NOT sound like:**
- Corporate: "We're excited to share our curated list of educational resources."
- Preachy: "Every parent has a responsibility to explore these tools."
- Vague: "AI is changing education in many exciting ways."

**Key voice attributes:**
- First person ("I tried..." not "Parents should try...")
- Specific (real tool names, real prices, real experiences)
- Honest about failures ("This didn't work for us, here's why")
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences)
- Em dashes for pace
- No hedging — direct statements

## 6. CONTENT AUTOMATION SYSTEM

### How Claude Powers the Content Machine

**The goal:** Ashley spends 30-45 minutes per week editing, not 3+ hours creating from scratch.

**The system:**

**Step 1: Weekly Content Brief (Claude generates)**
Every Sunday, TJ (or a VA) opens the Skip School Claude project and runs a prompt. Claude generates:
- Trending homeschool topics this week
- New AI education tools or updates
- ESA/school choice news
- Popular discussions in homeschool communities
- Suggested angles for the week

**Step 2: Article Outlines (Claude generates)**
Based on the brief, Claude creates 5-6 article skeleton outlines. Each outline includes:
- Hook (first 1-2 sentences)
- Main points (3-5 bullets)
- Ashley's angle (how this connects to her personal experience)
- Key stats or quotes to include
- Suggested CTA

**Step 3: Full Drafts (Claude generates)**
For the top 2-3 outlines, Claude writes complete first drafts in Ashley's voice. These are 80-90% ready. They include research, stats, tool recommendations, and practical advice.

**Step 4: Google Doc Output (Claude creates)**
Claude saves everything to a shared Google Doc (Google Drive integration). Ashley opens the Doc, picks the articles she wants to run, edits them for voice and personal anecdotes, and they're ready to schedule.

**Step 5: Ashley's Edit Pass (30-45 min/week)**
Ashley reads the drafts, changes anything that doesn't sound like her, adds personal stories or experiments with her kids, fact-checks anything that feels off, and finalizes. This is the human layer that makes the content authentic.

### What Claude Can and Cannot Do

**Claude handles well:**
- Research synthesis (finding trending topics, new tools, policy updates)
- First draft generation (structured, informative, on-brand)
- Reformatting national content for local editions (adding WilCo framing)
- ESA/policy research (state-by-state guides)
- Curriculum and tool comparisons (feature lists, pricing, pros/cons)

**Claude cannot replace:**
- Ashley's personal anecdotes and experiments
- Authentic voice that comes from actually homeschooling
- Editorial judgment about what matters this week
- Community relationship building
- Local knowledge (which co-ops are good, which events are worth attending)

### Voice Calibration

Before the content system goes live, Ashley needs to write 2-3 pieces organically (no AI assist) so Claude can learn her cadence, humor, sentence structure, and vocabulary. These become the voice reference files that the Claude project uses to generate on-brand drafts.

## 9. FRANCHISE MODEL

### The Vision

Skip School local editions are operated by homeschool parents who are already active in their local communities. They're not starting from zero — they already know the co-ops, the Facebook groups, the enrichment programs, the events. Skip School gives them a platform, a brand, and a content engine to formalize what they're already doing informally.

### Why This Franchise Model Works

**Low barrier to entry:** The local operator only needs to produce 30% of the content. The other 70% (AI tools, curriculum reviews, general homeschool content) is fed from the national brand. Compare that to WilCo Guide franchises where the local operator creates most of the content.

**Built-in network effects:** Homeschool parents are inherently networked through co-ops, Facebook groups, and meetups. A single enthusiastic local operator can drive hundreds of signups through word of mouth alone.

**Advertiser demand already exists:** Every community has tutoring centers, enrichment programs, co-ops, and educational services that want to reach homeschool families. The local operator just needs to connect them.

**ESA funding is spreading:** As more states pass school choice legislation, the pool of homeschool families with $10K+ per child to spend grows. Advertisers will increasingly pay to reach them.

### Ideal Local Operator Profile

- Active homeschool parent (currently homeschooling at least one child)
- Already embedded in local homeschool community (knows co-ops, groups, events)
- Comfortable with social media and basic tech
- Has 5-10 hours/week to dedicate
- Genuinely passionate about helping other homeschool families
- NOT required: Marketing experience, writing ability, tech skills (we provide all of this)

### What Local Operators Get

**From Skip School National:**
- Brand and visual identity (logo, templates, guidelines)
- 70% of newsletter content (pre-written, ready to insert)
- Beehiiv publication setup and training
- Meta ads playbook and creative templates
- Content automation system (Claude project access)
- Monthly training calls
- Community of other local operators
- Revenue share structure (details TBD)

**What Local Operators Provide:**
- 30% local content curation (events, co-ops, enrichment programs, local stories)
- Local Facebook group presence and community building
- Local advertiser relationships (eventually)
- Authentic local voice and knowledge

### Franchise Economics (Rough Projections)

**Local Operator Investment:**
- Upfront: $0-$2,500 (TBD based on what's included)
- Ongoing: Revenue share (10-20%) OR flat monthly fee ($200-$500)
- Meta ad budget: $100-$300/month (operator's cost)

**Local Operator Revenue Potential (Year 1):**
- Newsletter sponsorships: $500-$2,000/mo
- Directory listings: $250-$1,000/mo
- Affiliate revenue: $100-$500/mo
- Total: $1,000-$3,500/mo by month 6-12

**Timeline to Profitability:**
- Month 1-2: Building audience (500-1,500 subscribers)
- Month 3-4: First sponsorships and affiliate revenue
- Month 6: Break even on ad spend
- Month 9-12: Profitable ($1,000-$3,000/mo net)

### Expansion Roadmap

1. **Prove in WilCo** (Months 1-6): Validate the model, refine content system, test monetization
2. **Launch 2-3 pilot locations** (Months 6-9): Recruit local operators in Texas cities (Austin, Houston, San Antonio)
3. **Refine franchise package** (Months 9-12): Document what works, create training materials, standardize onboarding
4. **Scale nationally** (Year 2): 10-20 locations, focus on states with ESA programs
5. **Long-term vision** (Year 3+): 50+ locations, national brand recognition, info products, community platform

## 12. CRITICAL RULES & DON'TS

### What We MUST Do

1. **Lead with AI + Education positioning.** This is the differentiator. Every other homeschool newsletter covers curriculum and socialization. We cover that AND the AI revolution in education. That's why people choose us.

2. **Keep Ashley's voice authentic.** The content system exists to save time, not to replace Ashley. Every piece that goes out should feel like it came from a real homeschool mom, because it did. AI generates the research and structure; Ashley adds the soul.

3. **Be practical, not philosophical.** "Here's exactly how to use Claude to create a spelling list for your 6-year-old" beats "AI is transforming education" every time. Show, don't tell. Real tools, real steps, real results.

4. **Respect the homeschool community.** These parents made a hard, countercultural choice. They don't want to be talked down to. They don't want corporate-speak. They want another parent who gets it.

5. **Track ESA legislation obsessively.** This is the single biggest economic driver for our advertisers and our audience. When a new state passes school choice, that's a new market for local editions AND new content for the national newsletter.

6. **Mobile-first everything.** Non-negotiable. Moms are reading this on their phones while their kids do independent work.

### What We MUST NOT Do

- **Never be political about education.** We are not anti-public-school. We are pro-homeschool. There's a massive difference. No culture war content. No teacher-bashing. No partisan takes on education policy. Just practical help for families who chose this path.

- **Never over-promise AI capabilities.** AI is a powerful tool, not a replacement for parenting or teaching. Be honest about limitations. "Claude helped me create a great history outline, but I still had to teach the lesson" is more trustworthy than "AI does all the work for you."

- **Never share franchise/expansion plans publicly.** Just like WilCo Guide, the franchise model is internal strategy. Publicly, we're "a newsletter for homeschool families." Period.

- **Never use corporate or ed-tech jargon.** No "learning outcomes," "pedagogical frameworks," "student-centered paradigms." Talk like a parent, not an administrator.

- **Never spam homeschool Facebook groups.** Organic, genuine engagement only. Answer questions, share helpful resources, be a real community member. The moment you start posting newsletter links without context, you get banned and lose credibility.

- **Never publish AI-generated content without Ashley's edit pass.** Every piece goes through human review. No exceptions. One robotic-sounding newsletter destroys trust with this audience.

- **Never make medical or therapeutic claims about AI.** AI tools can help with education, not diagnose learning disabilities or replace professional assessments. Be clear about boundaries.
