01 · Brand Identity

Who We Are

Skip School is a national media brand at the intersection of AI and homeschool education. We help homeschool parents navigate AI tools, find local resources, and connect with a community that makes homeschooling easier and more effective. The brand is run by TJ and Ashley Larkin, with Ashley as the primary voice and face of the brand.

Tagline
The AI Playbook for Homeschool Parents
Brand Descriptor
Skip the classroom. Use AI to personalize how your kids learn. Give them a five-year head start.
One-Sentence Pitch
Skip School delivers weekly, practical guidance on using AI tools to homeschool your kids better, covering everything from curriculum to community to the money that makes it all possible.
One-Paragraph Pitch
Three massive forces are converging right now: homeschooling rates have tripled since COVID and continue to climb, AI tools are making it possible for any parent to deliver a world-class education at home, and states across the country are putting real money (up to $10,000 per child per year) directly into parents' hands through Education Savings Accounts. Skip School exists at the intersection of all three. Every week, we show homeschool parents exactly how to use AI to teach their kids, review the tools and curricula that actually work, break down ESA legislation in plain language, and connect families to local resources like co-ops, enrichment programs, and events. No jargon. No theory. Just practical help from a real homeschool family that uses this stuff every day.
Brand Name Origin
The name is intentionally provocative and memorable. Homeschool parents literally chose to skip the traditional school system. The .ai domain reinforces the AI-first positioning. SkipSchool.ai tells the entire brand story in two words and a domain extension.

Logo Rules

Do
Use on white or light cream backgrounds for maximum contrast.

Maintain clear space around the logo equal to the height of the "S" in SKIP.

Use the stacked version for square formats (social avatars, app icons).

Use the horizontal version for headers, email signatures, and wide formats.
Don't
Never place the standard logo on busy photographs or patterns.

Never change the yellow/navy color relationship.

Never stretch, rotate, or distort the logo.

Never add drop shadows, gradients, or effects to the logo.

05 · Voice & Tone

Ashley's Voice

All content is written as Ashley Larkin, a homeschool mom in Texas who uses AI tools with her kids and shares what she learns. Warm, practical, encouraging but not preachy.

What It Sounds Like

✓ This
"This week I let Claude write my 7-year-old's spelling list. Here's what happened."
✓ This
"Three apps we're actually using right now (and two I deleted after a week)."
✓ This
"I spent $0 on curriculum this month. Here's exactly how."

What It Does NOT Sound Like

✗ Not This
"We're excited to share our curated list of educational resources."
✗ Not This
"Every parent has a responsibility to explore these tools."
✗ Not This
"AI is changing education in many exciting ways."

Voice Rules

PerspectiveFirst person always. "I tried..." not "Parents should try..."
SpecificityReal tool names, real prices, real experiences. "Claude Sonnet" not "AI tools."
Honesty"This didn't work for us, here's why" builds more trust than only sharing wins.
ParagraphsShort. 1 to 3 sentences max. Let the writing breathe.
ToneNo hedging. "This tool is great for math" not "This tool could potentially help."
StyleConversational. Write like Ashley is texting a friend.
ApproachShow, don't tell. "Here's the exact prompt I used" beats "AI prompts can be helpful."

Banned Words

Kill these on sight. They make content sound like AI wrote it.

delvecruciallandscapeleveragerobustseamlessutilizeharnessholisticsynergyparadigmcutting-edgeecosystemunlockrevolutionizepedagogicalframeworkgame-changer
06 · Content Pillars

What We Cover

All content maps to four pillars. National newsletter is 70% of the content; local editions add 30% locally curated material.

40%
AI Tools for Homeschool
How to use Claude, ChatGPT, Khan Academy AI, Alpha School. Practical tutorials. Tool reviews. Ashley's real experiments.
25%
Curriculum & Resources
Curriculum reviews. Book recommendations by age. Educational app reviews. Free resource roundups.
15%
ESA / School Choice Money
State-by-state ESA guides. How to get the money, what it covers. Policy updates in plain language.
20%
Community & Lifestyle
Socialization strategies. Co-op spotlights. Day-in-the-life features. Field trip ideas.

Content Rules

We Cover
AI tools and how to use them for teaching.
Curriculum reviews with honest opinions.
ESA legislation and what it means for families.
Local co-ops, enrichment programs, events.
Real stories from real homeschool families.
Practical, copy-paste AI prompts.
We Don't Cover
Anti-public-school content or culture war takes.
Teacher bashing or partisan education policy.
Over-promising what AI can do.
Medical or therapeutic claims about AI.
Ed-tech jargon.
Franchise or expansion plans publicly.
07 · Newsletter Format

Issue Structure

Two newsletters per week. National on Wednesdays, local (WilCo) on Fridays. Both at 7am CT.

National Edition (Wednesdays)

1. Personal Hook2 to 3 sentences from Ashley about what happened this week.
2. Main ArticleOne deep piece from the pillars. 400 to 800 words.
3. Quick Hits2 to 3 short items: new tool, stat, or resource.
4. This Week's PromptOne copy-paste-ready AI prompt.
5. CTAShare, reply, or check out a resource.

WilCo Edition (Fridays)

1. Local HookWhat's happening in WilCo this week.
2. National RemixWednesday's article with local framing.
3. EventsCo-op meetings, enrichment, local events.
4. Local SpotlightFeature a WilCo family, co-op, or resource.
5. CTAShare, attend an event, reply.
