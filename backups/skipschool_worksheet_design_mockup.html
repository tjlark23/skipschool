The Garden Plan

Sophia stared at the empty patch of dirt behind the house and sighed. Every year, her grandmother grew tomatoes, peppers, and herbs in that spot. But Grandma had moved to a smaller apartment in January, and the garden sat untouched.

"I want to try," Sophia told her mom one Saturday morning.

Her mom raised an eyebrow. "Gardening is harder than it looks. You have to water every day, pull weeds, and be patient."

Sophia nodded. She rode her bike to the library and checked out two books about vegetable gardens. She spent the whole afternoon reading about soil, sunlight, and spacing. The next morning, she woke up early and started digging.

Three weeks later, tiny green sprouts pushed through the soil. Sophia took a photo and sent it to Grandma.

"You did the hard part," Grandma texted back. "You started."

By July, Sophia had more tomatoes than her family could eat. She filled a basket and left it on her neighbor's porch with a note: From Sophia's Garden.

1. What is the THEME of this story?
A. Gardens need a lot of water to grow.
B. Hard work and taking initiative lead to success.
C. Grandmothers are always the best gardeners.
D. Libraries have books about everything.
2. How does Sophia PREPARE before she starts gardening?
A. She asks her grandmother to come help.
B. She buys all the supplies at a store.
C. She goes to the library and reads about gardening.
D. She watches videos online about growing vegetables.
3. Why does Grandma say "You did the hard part"?
A. Because digging is the most physical work.
B. Because getting started is often the hardest step.
C. Because Sophia had to ride her bike to the library.
D. Because three weeks is a long time to wait.
4. What does the word "untouched" most likely mean in this story?
A. Broken and damaged
B. Perfectly cleaned
C. Not worked on or cared for
D. Covered in flowers
5. Which detail BEST shows that Sophia is generous?
A. She sent a photo to Grandma.
B. She read two books from the library.
C. She woke up early to start digging.
D. She left a basket of tomatoes on her neighbor's porch.
★ Go back to the story to find evidence for your answer. Don't just guess from memory! ★
