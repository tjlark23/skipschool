# Focus Area Problem Banks

When generating Tier 2 supplements, use these focus-area-specific patterns.
Each focus area has a recommended page structure and problem types.

## MATH FOCUS AREAS

### Addition/Subtraction (Grades 1-2)
- Number bonds and part-part-whole models
- Missing addend problems: ___ + 5 = 12
- Fact family triangles
- Vertical format with regrouping practice
- Real-world story problems (shopping, sharing, collecting)
- Mental math strategies (making 10, doubles, near-doubles)

### Place Value (Grades 1-3)
- Expanded form: 345 = 300 + 40 + 5
- Comparing with >, <, = using close numbers
- Rounding to nearest 10, 100, 1000
- Number puzzles: "I have 3 hundreds, 7 tens, and 2 ones. What number am I?"
- Base-ten block representation (described textually)
- Ordering numbers from least to greatest and vice versa

### Multiplication (Grades 3-5)
- Fact fluency drills by family (focus on struggle facts: 6x7, 7x8, 8x9)
- Array models with missing factors
- Properties of multiplication (commutative, associative, distributive)
- Estimation before solving
- Multi-digit multiplication with area model
- Real-world multiplication scenarios (equal groups, repeated addition)

### Division (Grades 3-5)
- Relationship to multiplication (if 6x7=42, then 42/7=6)
- Division with remainders and interpretation
- Long division steps with scaffolded practice
- Equal sharing story problems
- Checking division with multiplication
- Estimating quotients

### Fractions (Grades 1-5)
- Grade 1-2: Equal parts identification, halves and fourths only, shading fractions
- Grade 3: Naming, number line placement, comparing with same numerator or denominator
- Grade 4: Equivalent fractions, adding/subtracting with like denominators, mixed numbers
- Grade 5: Adding/subtracting unlike denominators, multiplying fractions, fraction-to-decimal conversion
- Visual models throughout (fraction bars, number lines, area models described textually)

### Time (Grades 1-3)
- Clock reading practice (analog only, described textually for PDF)
- Matching analog to digital
- Elapsed time number line method
- Schedule-based problems ("School starts at 8:15. Lunch is at 11:45. How long until lunch?")
- AM/PM understanding
- Real-world time word problems

### Money (Grades 1-3)
- Skip counting by coin values
- Making exact amounts with fewest coins
- Making change (count up method)
- Comparing prices and finding better deals
- Multi-step money word problems
- Decimal notation for money (Grade 2+)

### Geometry (Grades 1-5)
- Grade 1-2: Shape identification, attributes (sides, corners), real-world shape hunt
- Grade 3: Area and perimeter (rectangle focus), classifying shapes by attributes
- Grade 4: Angles (right, acute, obtuse), parallel and perpendicular lines, symmetry
- Grade 5: Coordinate graphing, classifying triangles and quadrilaterals, volume
- Composite shape problems (Grade 3+)

### Word Problems (All Grades)
- Operation identification practice ("Should I add, subtract, multiply, or divide?")
- Key word analysis (with caveat that key words aren't always reliable)
- Two-step problems with scaffolding (Grade 3+)
- Strip diagrams / bar models as problem-solving tools
- Problems with extra information (student must identify what's relevant)
- Problems with missing information ("What else do you need to know?")

### Data & Graphs (Grades 1-5)
- Grade 1-2: Picture graphs, tally charts, sorting data
- Grade 3: Bar graphs, frequency tables, dot plots
- Grade 4: Stem-and-leaf plots, interpreting data
- Grade 5: Mean, median, mode, range, scatterplots
- Creating graphs from data tables
- Drawing conclusions from graphs

## ELA FOCUS AREAS

### Phonics (Grades K-2)
- CVC word families with word sorts
- Digraph and blend discrimination
- Vowel team patterns with fill-in-the-blank sentences
- Silent e word transformation chains
- Dictation-ready word lists (parent reads, child writes)
- Nonsense word decoding (tests pure phonics, not memorization)

### Sight Words (Grades K-2)
- Read, write, use in context cycle
- Fill-in-the-blank sentences requiring sight word selection
- Sight word searches in short passages
- Spelling practice (look-cover-write-check format)
- Progressive lists (Dolch or Fry, grade-appropriate)

### Reading Comprehension (Grades 1-5)
- Additional passages (fiction and nonfiction) with STAAR-style questions
- Graphic organizer practice (story map, main idea web, Venn diagram described textually)
- Evidence-based answer practice ("Which sentence from the passage supports your answer?")
- Inference practice: "The author doesn't say it directly, but you can figure out that..."
- Summarizing practice: "Write a 2-3 sentence summary"
- Making connections: text-to-self, text-to-text, text-to-world

### Grammar (Grades 1-5)
- Parts of speech sorting and identification in context
- Subject-verb agreement practice
- Pronoun-antecedent agreement
- Comma rules (lists, compound sentences, introductory elements)
- Quotation marks in dialogue
- Sentence types (declarative, interrogative, exclamatory, imperative)
- Editing paragraphs with targeted error types

### Writing (Grades 1-5)
- Grade 1-2: Sentence expansion ("The dog ran." becomes "The big brown dog ran quickly through the muddy yard.")
- Grade 2-3: Paragraph organization (topic sentence, details, closing sentence)
- Grade 3-5: Essay structure (introduction, body paragraphs, conclusion)
- Grade 3-5: Writing in response to reading (text-dependent analysis)
- Descriptive writing with sensory details
- Narrative writing with dialogue
- Opinion/persuasive writing with reasons and evidence

### Spelling (Grades 1-5)
- Pattern-based word lists (not random)
- Look-say-cover-write-check format
- Word sorts by spelling pattern
- Spelling in context (fill-in-the-blank sentences)
- Commonly misspelled words for the grade level
- Homophones practice (their/there/they're, to/too/two, etc.)

### Vocabulary (Grades 2-5)
- Context clues practice with multiple-choice
- Word parts analysis (roots, prefixes, suffixes)
- Multiple meaning words in sentences
- Synonym/antonym matching
- Grade-level academic vocabulary (Tier 2 words)
- Using new vocabulary in original sentences

## SUPPLEMENT PAGE STRUCTURE

A Tier 2 supplement is 3-4 pages. Structure depends on the focus area:

**Single Math Focus (e.g., "Fractions"):**
- Page 1: Targeted practice (20-30 problems, scaffolded difficulty)
- Page 2: Visual models and conceptual understanding
- Page 3: Word problems applying the focus area
- Page 4 (optional): Challenge / extension problems

**Single ELA Focus (e.g., "Reading Comprehension"):**
- Page 1: Additional passage + questions
- Page 2: Strategy practice (graphic organizer, evidence hunting)
- Page 3: Application (writing in response to reading, or vocabulary from the passage)

**Dual Focus (e.g., "Fractions" + "Reading Comprehension"):**
- Pages 1-2: Math focus
- Pages 3-4: ELA focus

The supplement should go DEEPER than the base packet on the focus area.
If the base packet has 6 fraction problems, the supplement might have 20,
progressing from concrete to abstract.
