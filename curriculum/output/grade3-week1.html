Reading Comprehension: Fiction

Read the story carefully, then answer the questions.
Name: Date:

The Longest Yard Sale

Marco had exactly $4.75 in his pocket when his mom pulled the car over. A hand-painted sign read: YARD SALE, EVERYTHING MUST GO. Tables lined the driveway, covered in books, old toys, and kitchen gadgets.

"Ten minutes," his mom said. Marco nodded and headed straight for the book table. He picked up a thick blue book with gold letters on the spine: The World Atlas of Oceans. The price sticker said $5.00.

Marco's stomach sank. He was twenty-five cents short. He started to put it back when the man behind the table said, "You like oceans?"

"I want to be a marine biologist," Marco said quietly.

The man smiled. "Tell you what. If you can name three oceans, it's yours for what you've got."

"Pacific, Atlantic, Indian," Marco said without hesitating.

The man laughed and handed him the book. "Sold. You'll need that where you're going."

1. What is Marco's MAIN problem in this story?
A. He doesn't have enough time to shop.
B. He can't find any books he likes.
C. He doesn't have enough money for the book he wants.
D. His mom won't let him buy anything.
2. Why does the man lower the price for Marco?
A. The book is damaged and not worth $5.00.
B. He wants to get rid of everything quickly.
C. He is impressed by Marco's knowledge and passion.
D. Marco's mom asked him for a discount.
3. What does "Marco's stomach sank" most likely mean?
A. He was hungry.
B. He felt disappointed.
C. He was feeling sick.
D. He was scared of the man.
4. What does the man mean when he says "You'll need that where you're going"?
A. Marco is about to go on a trip.
B. The book will help Marco on his path to becoming a marine biologist.
C. Marco should read it in the car.
D. The man wants Marco to return the book later.
5. Which word BEST describes Marco?
A. Lazy
B. Determined
C. Angry
D. Shy
📚
Try This at Home
After reading, write 3 sentences about a time YOU started something new. How did it feel at first? What helped you keep going?
★When a question asks WHY, look for cause-and-effect clues in the story.

Grade 3 · Week 1 · Extensions

This Week's Extensions

Real-world activities, field trips, and family challenges.
Name: Date:
Math: Place Value
Grocery Store Number Hunt
Next time you're at the store, find 5 items with prices over $10. Write down each price. What digit is in the tens place? The ones place? Challenge: find the most expensive item in the store and identify every digit's place value.
ELA: Reading and Writing
Yard Sale Story Sequel
Marco got his ocean book! What happens next? Write a short sequel (at least 6 sentences) about what Marco discovers in the book. Include at least one character, a setting, and a problem that gets solved.
ELA: Grammar Challenge
Parts of Speech Scavenger Hunt
Open any book to a random page. Find and write down: 5 nouns, 5 verbs, and 5 adjectives from that page. Bonus: can you find an adverb?
🌱 Field Trip Idea
Local Library: Nonfiction Exploration
Visit the library and find 3 nonfiction books about something that interests your child (animals, space, cooking, sports). Practice identifying the main idea of one chapter. Use the table of contents and index to find specific facts.
🏆 Family Challenge
Place Value War
Each player draws 5 cards from a deck (remove face cards, aces = 1). Arrange your cards to make the BIGGEST 5-digit number you can. Compare numbers. Who has the bigger number? Play 5 rounds. Keep score!
★Learning doesn't stop at the worksheet. The best lessons happen in the real world!
